## ✨ New

**Waveform players inside your notes.** The plain embedded audio players (`![[demo.mp3]]`) are now replaced with a compact waveform wired to the very same player as the sidebar — no more bare grey control bar. Click the wave to play from anywhere, drag to select an A-B loop, double-click to drop your work marker — all in place, right in the note.

- **One player, everywhere.** The embed you're playing shows the live playhead and its button mirrors play/pause; the others stay as a static waveform (with their saved marker and loop) until you tap them. Only one track plays at a time, and the marker, loop zone, play counts and volume all stay in sync with the sidebar.
- **Reading view and Live Preview** — both are covered.
- **Light on resources.** A file's waveform is analyzed only once its player scrolls into view, the peak cache is shared with the sidebar (one decode per file), and idle waveforms park their redraw loop — so a note full of demos stays snappy.
- **New toggle "Waveform players in notes"** (Settings → Songwriter Player, on by default). Turn it off to get the plain player back plus the "open externally" button, exactly as before. Fully localized (EN/RU).

## 🐛 Fixes

- The waveform animation loop now suspends itself when nothing is moving, instead of redrawing at 60fps while idle.
